history

Early Origins: Whitewater rafting traces its earliest roots to ancient civilizations. Egyptians used papyrus rafts for transportation, while other cultures built log rafts to navigate rivers for travel and trade. In the 19th century, explorers like Lieutenant John Fremont and Horace H. Day experimented with inflatable rafts, setting the stage for modern rafting techniques.

Modern Growth: By the mid-20th century, rafting expanded rapidly through commercial trips and improved equipment. Today, it is a global adventure sport emphasizing safety, eco-friendly practices, and recreation. Pioneering regions such as California helped shape the commercial rafting industry, making it accessible and popular worldwide.
